# Sprint Frontend IA Decision Record

## Multi-agent Review Summary

- Information Architect: split the desktop into fast recognition, a persistent selected-candidature canvas, and a right context rail instead of a wall of equal panels.
- Interaction Designer: Smart View must remain fast and stable for calls; Detailed View owns complete editing.
- Accessibility Reviewer: use readable structure, stable focus targets, and no hover-only critical data.
- Local-first Frontend Engineer: wx desktop is the canonical v1 human runtime; avoid heavy dependencies and browser/server UI assumptions.
- Agent-workflow Designer: keep raw-offer intake, bounded tasks, CLI/descriptor discovery, and provider-free queue rules.
- Contrarian Reviewer: avoid generated bytecode artifacts, overengineering, and provider coupling.

## Final Decisions

- The wx desktop is the normal local working surface.
- Smart View is for fast recognition and call support, not broad CRUD.
- Detailed View owns complete candidature editing.
- User View owns editable professional/career profile data.
- New opportunities enter through raw-offer intake and later typed-field refinement.
- Keyword selection updates right-context material without losing the selected candidature.
- Agent work remains deterministic, bounded, and local-first.
